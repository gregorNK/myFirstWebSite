Rather than an inline-six, which has powered every other M3 in history, with the exception of the E30, the E92 BMW M3 has a 4.0 liter naturally-aspirated V8. ... For non-owners, it's the greatest M3 engine of all, as its a naturally-aspirated V8 that revs past 8,000 rpm and makes the most glorious noise of any M3.

In the regular SRT Hellcat, peak outputs are 717 hp and 656 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a six-speed manual transmission or an eight-speed TorqueFlite automatic.
This engine produces a maximum power of 457 PS (451 bhp - 336 kW) at 6800 rpm and a maximum torque of 600 Nm (442 lb.ft) at 5000 rpm. The power is transmitted to the road by the rear wheel drive (RWD) with a 7 speed Automatic gearbox.
